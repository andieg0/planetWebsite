THE SOLAR SYSTEM

Facts ...
Formation: 4.6 billion years ago
It has:
  • one star;
  • eight planets;
  • five officially recognized dwarf planets;
  • at least 290 moons.
  • more than 1.3 million asteroids;
  • about 3,900 comets.
Location: Orion Spur - an outer spiral arm of the Milky Way Galaxy.
Speed: It orbits the center of the galaxy at about 515,000 mph (828,000 kph).
Orbit Duration: about 230 million years.
Explore more: See the Solar System

MERCURY

Name Origin: Named after the fastest of the ancient Roman gods.
Size: 3,032 miles (4,879 kilometers)
Type: Terrestrial
Mean Temperature: 333°F (167°C)
Distance from the Sun: average distance of 36 million miles (58 million kilometers).
Orbit Duration: 88 Earth Days
Day Duration:: 59 Earth days
Speed: 29.4 miles (47.4 kilometers) per second.
Other Facts:
  • Mercury is the nearest to the Sun planet.
  • It is the smallest planet in the Solar System.
  • It is the fastest planet.
  • It is the second densest planet after the Earth: 85% of its radius is taken by a big metallic core.
  • It orbits almost upright around the Sun.
