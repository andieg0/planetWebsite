Mercury - The Swift Planet
Other Facts:
  • Mercury is the nearest to the Sun planet.
  • It is the smallest planet in the Solar System.
  • It is the fastest planet.
  • It is the second densest planet after the Earth: 85% of its radius is taken by a big metallic core.
  • It orbits almost upright around the Sun.

Venus - Morning and Evening Star
Other Facts:
  • Venus spins slowly in the opposite direction from most planets.
  • It the hottest planet in our solar system because of its thick atmosphere.
  • It is the only planet named after a female god.
  • At Earth, Venus is seen to be the brightest object in the night sky after the Moon. They both have phases – full, half, quarter, etc. but Venus completes the cycle for 584 Earth days.
  • It has an induced magnetic field, not internally generated.

Earth - The Blue Planet
Other Facts:
  • Earth is the biggest of the terrestrial planets and the fifth largest planet overall.
  • Earth is the only planet that has a single moon.
  • It the only planet in the Solar System with liquid water on the surface.
  • The only known place so far with population of living things.
  • Magnetic polarity of Earth can reverse at about every 400,000 years on average without harming life on the planet.

Mars - The Red Planet
Other Facts:
  • Mars has two small moons - Phobos and Deimos, looking like potatoes.
  • Mars can easily be spotted in the night sky because of its red colour.
  • It is one of the most explored bodies in the Solar System.
  • It has the largest volcano in the Solar System, Olympus Mons - three times taller than Earth's Mt. Everest.
  • It has no global magnetic field now but parts of its southern hemisphere show traces of a magnetic field from 4 billion years ago.
More
Jupiter - The Gas Giant
Other Facts:
  • Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System.
  • It has the shortest day in the Solar System.
  • Its enormous magnetic field is 16 to 54 times more powerful than that of the Earth.
  • It has the largest ocean in the Solar System which is made of hydrogen instead of water.
  • It has 95 officially recognized moons and faint ring system.
More
Saturn - The Ringed Planet
Other Facts:
  • Saturn is the furthest planet in the Solar System that can be seen by the unaided human eye from Earth.
  • As of June 8, 2023, Saturn has 146 officially confirmed moons in its orbit.
  • It has spectacular ring system where each ring orbits at a different speed around the planet..
  • Saturn's north pole has an interesting atmospheric feature – a six-sided jet stream.
  • It is the only planet in the Solar System with an average density that is less than water.
More
Uranus - The Bull's Eye
Other Facts:
  • Uranus was the first planet discovered with the aid of a telescope. .
  • It is surrounded by 13 faint rings and 27 small moons..
  • It is the only planet whose equator has a tilt of 97.77 degrees to its orbit and seems to rotate sideways.
  • Due to its unique tilt Uranus has the most extreme seasons in the Solar System. The Sun shines directly over one of the poles for almost 21 Earth years, while the other half of the planet is covered by a dark winter.
  • It rotates in the opposite direction than most of the planets.
More
Neptune - The Big Blue Planet
Other Facts:
  • Neptune is the first planet located through mathematical calculations.
  • It is the only planet in our solar system not visible to the unaided human eye.
  • It is the furthest planet in the Solar System.
  • It has 14 known moons.
  • Neptune has at least five main rings and four prominent ring arcs.
  • Neptune is the Solar System's windiest world. The wind speed can reach more than 1,200 miles per hour (2,000 kilometers per hour).
  • Neptune is the densest one of the giant planets.
More
Pluto - The Dwarf Planet
Other Facts:
  • It is the only planet named by an 11-year-old girl
  • It has five known moons: Charon, Nix, Hydra, Kerberos, and Styx.
  • its axis of rotation is tilted 57 degrees against it so it spins nearly sideways.
  • Pluto's orbit around the Sun is oval and makes it switch places with Neptune for 20 years every 248 Earth years.
  • It has heart-shaped glacier.
