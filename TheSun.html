SUN

Name Origin: Related to:
  • Latin word “Sol” – for sun-related adjectives
  • Helios (the ancient Greek God of the sun) – for sun- related terminoligy.
Size: 435,000 miles (700,000 kilometers).
Type: G2 V, yellow dwarf main-sequence star
Mean Temperature:
  1. The core – more than 27 million °F (15 million °C)
  2. The photosphere - 10,000 °F (5,500 °C)
  3. The corona - up to 3.5 million °F (2 million °C)
Distance: 93 million miles (150 million kilometers) from the Earth.
Orbit Duration: 230 million years to complete an orbit around the Milky Way.
Day Duration:: The Sun is not solid and its parts rotate with different speed:
  1. At the equator: 25 Earth days
  2. At the poles: 36 Earth days
Speed: 450,000 miles per hour (720,000 kilometers per hour).
Other Facts:
  • Its gravity holds the Solar System together and keep everything in orbit around it.
  • It is the largest object in the Solar System, 100 times wider than the Earth.
  • Its geographic poles N and S change their polarity at almost every 11 years.
  • The corona is much hotter than the layer below it.
  • The alignment of the Sun, the Moon and the Earth results in eclipses.
